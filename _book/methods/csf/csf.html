3  Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF)

Authors Suzanne Schindler, MD, PhD1, Fatima S. Amtashar, MS2, Rachel Henson, MS3, Elizabeth Herries, BA1, and Jasmine Stewert, BS1

Affiliations

  1. Knight Alzheimer Disease Research Center Fluid Biomarker Core Department of Neurology, Ladenson Lab Department of Pathology and Immunology, Washington University in St. Louis

Summary or Scientific Abstract. CSF samples were processed and stored at the Knight ADRC Fluid Biomarker Core and pulled in two sets on 10/19/2023. With one aliquot, concentrations of Aβ40, Aβ42, total tau (t-tau), tau phosphorylated at 181 (p-tau181) and neurofilament light chain (NfL), were measured by chemiluminescent enzyme immunoassay using a fully automated platform (LUMIPULSE G1200, Fujirebio, Malvern, PA) according to manufacturer’s specifications. All samples and controls were analyzed on 12/08/2023 in singlicate, and three pooled CSF controls were analyzed in duplicate for quality control (QC).

With a second aliquot, concentrations of Visinin-like Protein-1 (VILIP-1), Synaptosomal- Associated Protein [25KDa] (SNAP-25) and Neurogranin (Ng) were measured using quantitative fluorescent two-site immunoassays using Single Molecule Counting (SMCTM) technology originally marketed by Singulex for their Erenna® system. Samples were analyzed between 10/21/2023 and 10/28/2023. Samples were frozen and later used for the measurement of YKL-40 using a commercially available plate-based assay (Quidel MicroVueTM Bone) between 3/26/2024-4/2/2024. All controls and samples were analyzed in duplicate.

3.1 Method

Lumipulse: Prior to analysis, samples were brought to room temperature per manufacturer’s instructions.[1–4] Samples were briefly vortexed and centrifuged to collect, and 450ul was transferred to polystyrene cuvettes for analysis. Concentrations of p-tau181, t-tau, Aβ40, Aβ42 and NfL, were measured using the Lumipulse G1200 fully automated platform according to manufacturer’s specifications,[1–4] and analysis was completed within 4 hours of transfer off wet ice to maintain sample stability. Fujirebio recommends that each lab establish their own ranges which are reflected in table 1 alongside the manufacturer’s measurement ranges.

YKL-40: The MicroVue YKL-40 assay kit has been validated for use with human serum and EDTA plasma specimens by Quidel Corporation. Additionally, human cerebrospinal fluid has been tested and shown to be suitable for use in the assay in an independent study.[5,6] Concentrations of YKL-40 were measured according to manufacturer’s specifications.[5] Prior to analysis, CSF samples were thawed on wet ice and vortexed briefly to mix. Samples were diluted three-fold with the provided sample diluent in a 96-well pre-plate and mixed well prior to transferring to the assay plate. Samples, standards, and controls were run in duplicate. All incubation steps were run at room temperature (18 to 28⁰C). After an incubation period of 60 minutes, plates were washed to remove any unbound material. In the second step, the rabbit anti-YKL-40 detection antibody was added to each assay well, and the plate was incubated for 60 minutes. Plates were washed again and in the third step the chromogenic substrate solution was added to the assay wells for 60 minutes. After the incubation period, the reaction was stopped by adding manufacturer supplied Stop Solution and the optical density (OD) was read at 405 nm. The color intensity of the reaction mixture is proportional to the concentration of YKL-40 present in the test specimens, standards, and controls.[5] The limit of quantitation for this assay and detection range are reflected in table 2.

VILIP-1, SNAP-25 and Neurogranin: Prior to analysis, samples were thawed for one hour at 4°C and centrifuged at 11,000 g for 30 seconds. 200 ul of CSF was transferred to 96 well pre-plate. All assay steps were performed at room temperature (19 to 21 ⁰C).

For the VILIP-1, SNAP-25 and Ng assays, per well, 25ul, 25ul and 5ul of CSF respectively (or standard) were diluted with buffer to a total volume of 100ul, then combined with 100ul of capture antibody coated magnetic microparticles per well. Samples were assayed in duplicate and standards in triplicate. Plates were then mixed for 2 hours at ~500 rpms on a plate shaker. After washing the magnetic microparticles two times using a Tecan Hydroflex plate washer, 20ul of fluorescent dye labeled detection antibody was added and mixed for one hour at ~650 rpm. After washing the magnetic microparticles 4 times, 21ul per well of elution buffer was added for 10 minutes (with mixing at ~650 rpm) to separate detection antibodies from the microparticles. Eluted antibodies (20ul per well) were then transferred to an Aurora glass-bottomed barrier plate containing 4ul per well of Buffer C (pH = 8) to neutralize the elution buffer (pH = 2.5). After briefly pulse centrifuging the plate to collect and mix the well contents, plates were read on the SMCxPRO instrument.

Calibration curves for the VILIP-1, SNAP-25 and Ng assays were prepared with Nmyristoylated His-tagged Human VILIP-1 monomer from Proteos, Inc (Kalamazoo, MI), Cell Sciences (Newburyport, MA) recombinant human SNAP-25-His and synthetic full-length Ng from AAPPTec Inc (Louisville, KY) respectively. All calibration materials were quantitated using amino acid analysis. The methods for preparation of assay reagents and assay protocols are described in Crimmins 2017 Clinical Chemistry for VILIP-1,[7] Sutphen 2018 Alzheimer’s & Dementia for SNAP-25,[8] and Willemse 2018 Clinical Chemistry for Ng.[9] Lots for these assays are reflected in table 3. Limits of quantitation are shown in table 4 and QC measurements are reflected in table 5.

3.2 Data Dictionary

  1. BDS ID: BDS ID Assigned to samples.
  2. NiAD_ID: NiAD ID assigned to samples.
  3. ADDS_ID: ADDS ID assigned to samples.
  4. CSF_LP_DATE: Date that CSF was collected.
  5. Visit: Visit number as recorded on the CSF collection worksheet
  6. Cycle: Cycle number as recorded on the CSF collection worksheet
  7. LP_no: LP Number noted by Fluid Biomarker Core
  8. CSF_AB40: Aβ40 in pg/mL
  9. CSF_AB42: Aβ42 in pg/mL
  10. CSF_tTau: total tau in pg/mL
  11. CSF_pTau: tau phosphorylated at position 181 in pg/mL
  12. CSF_NfL: Neurofilament light chain in pg/mL
  13. CSF_YKL40: YKL-40 in ng/mL
  14. CSF_SNAP25: Synaptosomal-Associated Protein [25KDa] in pg/mL
  15. CSF_NG: Neurogranin in pg/mL
  16. CSF_VILIP1: Visinin-like Protein-1in pg/mL
