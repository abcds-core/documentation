GENOMIC DATA (GENOME-WIDE SNP MICROARRAY)

Authors Lam-Ha T. Dang1, M. Ilyas Kamboh2, Carlos Cruchaga3, and Joseph H. Lee1

Affiliations

Summary. This dataset includes genome-wide microarray data for 373 adults with Down Syndrome (DS) and 43 sibling non-DS controls (total N=416) in the Alzheimer’s Biomarkers Consortium — Down Syndrome (ABC-DS) study. This genomic data set includes data on study participants who were enrolled in the U01 part of the ABC-DS study as of 4/30/2022.

4.1 Genotyping

Genotyping was performed at the Center for Applied Genomics at Children’s Hospital of Pennsylvania using the Illumina Infinium General Screening Array (GSA) Version 2, with added disease markers for all samples, except for 25 participants for which Version 3 was used. In total, 759,993 and 654,027 variants across the genome were genotyped on GSAv2 and GSAv3, respectively. The two versions of the genotyping microarray had 619,856 (~95%) overlapping variants. We have provided several subsets of the complete data set (see “Summary of the data files provided” for more details). Variants in these data were mapped to the GRCh37/hg19 assembly.

4.2 Trisomic Chromosome 21

Chromosome 21 was treated separately because traditional tools cannot be applied to trisomic chromosome 21. Trisomic variants were treated as copy number variants (CNV) and were called in using the cnvPartition CNV Analysis (v3.2.0) plug-in in GenomeStudio 2.0 with Genotyping module.[1,2] Allele specific genotypes were then exported using the CNV Region Report plug-in (v.2.1.2)[2]. In total, 9,785 and 8,446 variants were genotyped on GSAv2 and GSAv3, respectively. As done commonly, the p-arm of Chr. 21 was dropped from analysis because it is known to be highly heterochromatic with numerous repeat sequences. In the current data, this characteristic resulted in high rate of variants called as disomic in among those karyotyped with DS and discrepancies between duplicates in this region. For these reasons, all variants genotyped on the p-arm should be excluded from analysis.

4.3 Genotype quality control

To maximize the flexibility of these data for all investigators, we aimed to minimize the likelihood of distributing erroneous data, while minimizing the quality control steps. For example, we included SNPs that did not achieve high genotyping rates, which would have been dropped in a standard analysis practice. Specifically, we checked for sex-mismatch cases by identifying cases where sex obtained from the analysis of X-chromosome SNPs using PLINK[3] did not match the reported sex. These cases were re-confirmed with research coordinators to rule out potential data entry error, etc. Relationships (e.g., siblings, monozygotic twins) among subjects were confirmed by pair-wise genome-wide estimates of proportion identity-by-descent (IBD) computed using the KING[4] software. Discrepant relationships were reviewed against available demographic and family data (when siblings are genotyped) to determine the most likely relationship consistent with the proportion of IBD or other scenarios (i.e., sample duplicates, duplicate enrollments across cohorts). Samples with unresolved or ambiguous identities arising from mismatched sex and discrepant relationships were excluded from the dataset. In duplicated samples, the samples with the lower genotyping call rate were dropped from the data set. In the end, data were provided for 416 participants.

4.4 Imputation

We are not providing imputed data and suggest the TOPMed Imputation Server for genotype imputation.

4.5 Summary of the data files

Nomenclature Subject Identification (ID) Number

  • For non-DS control siblings, the Individual ID (IID) of the DS-proband was used as the Family ID (FID)
  • For families consisting of multiple participants with DS, the lower valued IID was used as the FID
  • In data files related to Chromosome 21, participant IDs were formatted as follows: “FID_IID”

Trisomic Chromosome 21 genotypes

  • ABC-DS.LONI.Chr21.GRCh37.CNCounts.txt: Copy number count at each genotyped position.
  • ABC-DS.LONI.Chr21.GRCh37.Dosage.txt: Dosage of the “A” allele (“A_Allele” column). Dosage can be 0, 1, 2, or 3 copies.
  • ABC-DS.LONI.Chr21.GRCh37.Genotype.txt: Genotype in ACGT coding

Non-Chromosome 21 genotypes: These genotypes are provided in binary PLINK data files (i.e., bed/bim/fam files) with pedigree and sex information.

PLINK files consisting of all variants genotyped (i.e., the union of GSAv2 and GSAv3 chips)

  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.allSNPs.bed
  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.allSNPs.bim
  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.allSNPs.fam

PLINK files consisting of only overlapping variants (i.e., the intersection) between GSAv2 and GSAv3 chips

  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.bed
  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.bim
  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.fam

PLINK files consisting of the remaining non-overlapping variants

  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.nonOverlappingVars.bed
  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.nonOverlappingVars.bim
  • ABC-DS.GRCh37.non-Chr21.LONI.nonOverlappingVars.fam
