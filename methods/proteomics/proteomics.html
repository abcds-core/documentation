8.1 Background

Serum and plasma samples were processed and stored at the University of North Texas Health Science Center Institute for Translational Research and were run across three waves (Wave 1: 2019; Wave 2: 2021; Wave 3: 2022). Blood samples were initially prepared through an automated system utilizing a customized Hamilton Robotics StarPlus system. Proteomic assay kits were purchased from select vendors (Meso Scale Discovery, Quanterix). Samples were run in singlicate, with a subset run in duplicate. Control samples were included across plates and runs for quality control.

8.2 Methods

Electrochemiluminescence (ECL)

Proteomic assay kits were commercially obtained from Meso Scale Discovery (MSD; http://www.mesoscale.com). Per our previously published methods,[1,2] samples were assayed using electrochemiluminescence (ECL) technology. The ECL platform has been extensively applied to assay biomarkers associated with the range of neurodegenerative diseases including Alzheimer’s disease (AD). The UNTHSC laboratory maintains a database of over n>2000 samples from the neurotypical AD population conducted in duplicate using the ECL technology on the referenced MSD platform with average coefficient of variation (CVs) < 10%. A total of 500ul of plasma and serum were assayed for the following proteomic markers: fatty acid binding protein 3 (FABP3), beta 2 microblogulin (B2M), Factor VII, I-309, pancreatic polypeptide (PPY), c-reactive protein (CRP), thrombopoietin (TPO), interleukin (IL)-5, IL-6, IL-8, IL-10, IL-18, alpha 2 macroglobulin (A2M), eotaxin 3, vascular cell adhesion molecule-1 (sVCAM-1), soluble intercellular adhesion molecule-1 (sICAM-1), thymus and activation regulated chemokine (TARC), tenascin C, tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-a), and serum amyloid A (SAA) (see Table 1). Five pooled plasma and serum control samples were included on each MSD plate layout reflecting a similar distribution of age for Down Syndrome (DS) samples and sibling controls.

Single Molecular Array (SIMOA)

Proteomic assay kits were commercially obtained from Quanterix (http://quanterix.com). A total of 500 ul of plasma was assayed using Single Molecule Array (SIMOA) technology using HD-1 imager for some of Wave 1 assays and HD-X imager for Wave 1, Wave 2, and 3 (see Table 2). The following proteomic markers were derived: Amyloid beta (Aβ) 40 and 42, total tau, ptau181, glial fibrillary acidic protein (GFAP) and neurofilament light chain (NfL). Five pooled plasma control samples were included on each SIMOA plate layout reflecting a similar distribution of age for DS samples and sibling controls.

8.3 QA/QC

All supplies for key biological resources were purchased from multiple vendors via commercially available kits (e.g. Meso Scale Discovery; Quanterix). Each of these vendors conducts internal validation of resources. Protocols are conducted to ensure reproducibility of findings. Protocols include validation of resources, which spans monitoring dilution linearity, spiked recovery, monitoring of low and high end of detection ranges to avoid lack of reliability at restricted ranges, examination of matrix effects, inclusion of pooled standards across lots, examination of inter- and intra-assay variability, real-time monitoring of coefficient of variation (CVs) and monitoring of all lot numbers across experiments.
